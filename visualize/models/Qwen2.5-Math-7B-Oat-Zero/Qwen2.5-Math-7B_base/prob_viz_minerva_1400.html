For a semi-infinite solid with a constant initial concentration and no flux at the surface, the solution to Fick's second law is given by the error function (erf): \[ C(x,t) = C_0 \left[1 - \text{erf}\left(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{Dt}}\right)\right] \] where: - \( C(x,t) \) is the concentration at position \( x \) and time \( t \), - \( C_0 \) is the initial concentration, - \( D \) is the diffusion coefficient, - \( t \) is the time, - \( \text{erf} \) is the error function. We are given: - The initial concentration \( C_0 \), - The concentration at depth \( x \) after 10 minutes (600 seconds) is \( \frac{C_0}{3} \), - The diffusion coefficient \( D = 3.091 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{cm}^2/\text{s} \).
