This reduces the problem to arranging 7 "blocks" (6 girls + 1 block of 2 boys) in a row. 1. First, we calculate the number of ways to arrange the 7 "blocks". This is simply 7! (7 factorial). 2. Next, we need to consider the number of ways to arrange the 2 boys within their "block". There are 2! (2 factorial) ways to arrange the 2 boys.
