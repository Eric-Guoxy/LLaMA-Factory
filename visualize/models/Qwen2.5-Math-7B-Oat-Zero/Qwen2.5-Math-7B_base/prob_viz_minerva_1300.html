The rotor is subject to an applied torque \(T\) and two frictional torques from the bearings, each with a frictional coefficient \(B\). The moment of inertia of the rotor is \(I\). The term \(-2B\omega\) represents the total frictional torque from the two bearings. This is a first-order linear differential equation. To solve it, we can use the method of integrating factors.
