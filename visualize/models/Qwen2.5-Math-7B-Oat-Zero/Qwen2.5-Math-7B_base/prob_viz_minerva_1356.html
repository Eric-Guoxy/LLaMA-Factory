To determine the energy of a photon with a wavelength of 800 nm, we can use the formula for the energy of a photon: \[ E = \frac{hc}{\lambda} \] where: - \( E \) is the energy of the photon, - \( h \) is Planck's constant (\( 6.626 \times 10^{-34} \) J·s), - \( c \) is the speed of light (\( 3.00 \times 10^8 \) m/s), - \( \lambda \) is the wavelength of the photon (800 nm = \( 800 \times 10^{-9} \) m). First, we need to calculate the energy in joules and then convert it to electronvolts (eV). The conversion factor from joules to electronvolts is \( 1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J} \). Let's calculate this step-by-step using Python. ```python # Constants h = 6.626e-34 # Planck's constant in J·s c = 3.00e8 # speed of light in m/s lambda_ = 800e-9 # wavelength in meters # Energy of the photon in joules E_joules = (h * c) / lambda_ # Conversion factor from joules to electronvolts eV_per_joule = 1 / 1.602e-19 # Energy of the photon in electronvolts E_eV = E_joules * eV_per_joule E_eV_rounded = round(E_eV, 2) print(E_eV_rounded) ``` ```output 1.55 ``` The energy of a photon with a wavelength of 800 nm is \(\boxed{1.55}\) eV.
