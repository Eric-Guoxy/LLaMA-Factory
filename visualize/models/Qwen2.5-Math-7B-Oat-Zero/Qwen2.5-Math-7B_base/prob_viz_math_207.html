To determine how many different pizzas Mikka can order with two different toppings from a choice of 8 toppings, we need to calculate the number of combinations of 8 toppings taken 2 at a time. This is because the order in which the toppings are chosen does not matter. The formula for combinations is given by: \[ \binom{n}{r} = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} \] where \( n \) is the total number of items to choose from, \( r \) is the number of items to choose, and \(! \) denotes factorial, which is the product of all positive integers up to that number. In this problem, \( n = 8 \) and \( r = 2 \). Plugging these values into the formula, we get: \[ \binom{8}{2} = \frac{8!}{2!(8-2)!} = \frac{8!}{2!6!} \] Next, we simplify the factorials. Recall that \( 8! = 8 \times 7 \times 6! \). The \( 6! \) terms cancel out in the numerator and the denominator: \[ \binom{8}{2} = \frac{8 \times 7 \times 6!}{2! \times 6!} = \frac{8 \times 7}{2!} = \frac{8 \times 7}{2 \times 1} = \frac{56}{2} = 28 \] Therefore, the number of different pizzas Mikka can order is \(\boxed{28}\).
