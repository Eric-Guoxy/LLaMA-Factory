The forces acting on the mass are the tension in the rod, the gravitational force, and the external horizontal force \( f(t) \). 1. **Identify the forces:** - Tension in the rod, \( T \), which acts along the rod. - Gravitational force, \( mg \), which acts vertically downward. - External horizontal force, \( f(t) \), which acts horizontally. 2. **Resolve the forces into components:** - The tension \( T \) has no tangential component. - The gravitational force \( mg \) can be resolved into a tangential component \( mg \sin \theta \) and a radial component \( mg \cos \theta \). - The external force \( f(t) \) is already in the tangential direction. 3. **Apply Newton's second law in the tangential direction:** The tangential component of the net force is equal to the mass times the tangential acceleration. The tangential acceleration is \( l \ddot{\theta} \) (since the tangential velocity is \( l \dot{\theta} \) and the tangential acceleration is the derivative of the tangential velocity with respect to time). The tangential component of the net force is \( f(t) - mg \sin \theta \). Therefore, we have: \[ f(t) - mg \sin \theta = ml \ddot{\theta} \] 4. **Rearrange the equation:** \[ ml \ddot{\theta} + mg \sin \theta = f(t) \] 5. **Divide through by \( m \):** \[ l \ddot{\theta} + g \sin \theta = \frac{f(t)}{m} \] 6. **Write the final equation of motion:** \[ \boxed{l \ddot{\theta} + g \sin \theta = \frac{f(t)}{m}} \] This is the equation of motion for the pendulum.
