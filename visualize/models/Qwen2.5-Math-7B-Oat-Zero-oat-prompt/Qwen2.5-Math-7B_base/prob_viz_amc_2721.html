To solve the problem, we need to understand the effect of each transformation \( T_k \) on the point \((1, 0)\). The transformation \( T_k \) consists of two steps: 1. Rotate the point \( k \) degrees counterclockwise around the origin. 2. Reflect the resulting point across the \( y \)-axis. First, let's consider the effect of the rotation. Rotating the point \((1, 0)\) by \( k \) degrees counterclockwise around the origin results in the point \((\cos k^\circ, \sin k^\circ)\). Then, reflecting this point across the \( y \)-axis changes the \( x \)-coordinate to its negative, resulting in the point \((- \cos k^\circ, \sin k^\circ)\). So, the transformation \( T_k \) applied to the point \((1, 0)\) results in the point \((- \cos k^\circ, \sin k^\circ)\). We need to find the smallest positive integer \( n \) such that performing the sequence of transformations \( T_1, T_2, T_3, \ldots, T_n \) returns the point \((1, 0)\) back to itself. This means that after applying all these transformations, the point \((1, 0)\) should be transformed back to \((1, 0)\). The sequence of transformations can be described as follows: 1. \( T_1 \) transforms \((1, 0)\) to \((- \cos 1^\circ, \sin 1^\circ)\). 2. \( T_2 \) transforms \((- \cos 1^\circ, \sin 1^\circ)\) to \((- \cos 2^\circ, \sin 2^\circ)\). 3. \( T_3 \) transforms \((- \cos 2^\circ, \sin 2^\circ)\) to \((- \cos 3^\circ, \sin 3^\circ)\). 4. And so on, until \( T_n \) transforms \((- \cos (n-1)^\circ, \sin (n-1)^\circ)\) to \((- \cos n^\circ, \sin n^\circ)\). After \( n \) transformations, the point \((1, 0)\) is transformed to \((- \cos n^\circ, \sin n^\circ)\). For this to be equal to \((1, 0)\), we need: \[ - \cos n^\circ = 1 \quad \text{and} \quad \sin n^\circ = 0 \] This happens when \( n = 360^\circ \cdot k \) for some integer \( k \), but since we need the least positive integer \( n \), we take \( k = 1 \), so \( n = 360 \). The smallest positive multiple of 360 is 360 itself. Therefore, the least positive integer \( n \) such that performing the sequence of transformations \( T_1, T_2, T_3, \ldots, T_n \) returns the point \((1, 0)\) back to itself is \( \boxed{720} \).
