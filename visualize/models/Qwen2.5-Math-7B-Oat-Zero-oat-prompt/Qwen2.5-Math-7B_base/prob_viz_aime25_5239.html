This is a property of tangential quadrilaterals. - The area \(A\) of the trapezoid can be expressed in terms of the height \(h\) and the lengths of the parallel sides \(r\) and \(s\): \[ A = \frac{1}{2} \times (r + s) \times h \] - The radius \(R\) of the inscribed circle is given by: \[ R = \frac{h}{2} \] Since the radius \(R = 3\), we have \(h = 6\). 2. **Area of the Trapezoid:** - Given the area \(A = 72\), we can substitute \(h = 6\) into the area formula: \[ 72 = \frac{1}{2} \times (r + s) \times 6 \] Simplifying this, we get: \[ 72 = 3 \times (r + s) \implies r + s = 24 \] 3. **Relationship between the Sides:** - Since the trapezoid is isosceles and has an inscribed circle, the sum of the lengths of the parallel sides is equal to the sum of the lengths of the non-parallel sides.
