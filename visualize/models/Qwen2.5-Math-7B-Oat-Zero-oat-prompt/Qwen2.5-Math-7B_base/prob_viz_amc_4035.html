To solve this problem, we need to determine the probability that Flora the frog, starting at 0 on the number line, will eventually land at 10. The frog makes jumps to the right, with the probability of jumping a distance \( m \) being \( \frac{1}{2^m} \). This is a classic problem that can be approached using the concept of generating functions and infinite series. Let \( p_k \) be the probability that Flora will eventually land at \( k \). We are interested in finding \( p_{10} \). The key insight is to recognize that the probability \( p_k \) can be expressed in terms of the probabilities \( p_{k-m} \) for all possible jumps \( m \). Specifically, we have: \[ p_k = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2^m} p_{k-m} \] for \( k \geq 1 \), with the initial condition \( p_0 = 1 \) (since Flora is already at 0 with probability 1). To find \( p_{10} \), we can use the above recursive relation. However, it is more convenient to use the generating function approach. Let \( P(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} p_k x^k \) be the generating function for the probabilities \( p_k \). Multiplying both sides of the recursive relation by \( x^k \) and summing over all \( k \geq 1 \), we get: \[ \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} p_k x^k = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2^m} p_{k-m} x^k. \] The left-hand side is \( P(x) - p_0 = P(x) - 1 \) (since \( p_0 = 1 \)). The right-hand side can be rewritten as: \[ \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2^m} \sum_{k=m}^{\infty} p_{k-m} x^k = \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2^m} x^m \sum_{j=0}^{\infty} p_j x^j = \left( \sum_{m=1}^{\infty} \frac{x^m}{2^m} \right) P(x) = \frac{x}{2-x} P(x). \] Thus, we have: \[ P(x) - 1 = \frac{x}{2-x} P(x). \] Solving for \( P(x) \), we get: \[ P(x) \left( 1 - \frac{x}{2-x} \right) = 1 \implies P(x) \left( \frac{2-x-x}{2-x} \right) = 1 \implies P(x) \left( \frac{2-2x}{2-x} \right) = 1 \implies P(x) = \frac{2-x}{2-2x} = \frac{1}{1-x/2}. \] The generating function \( P(x) = \frac{1}{1-x/2} \) can be expanded as a geometric series: \[ P(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \left( \frac{x}{2} \right)^k. \] Thus, the coefficient of \( x^{10} \) in \( P(x) \) is \( \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{10} = \frac{1}{1024} \).
