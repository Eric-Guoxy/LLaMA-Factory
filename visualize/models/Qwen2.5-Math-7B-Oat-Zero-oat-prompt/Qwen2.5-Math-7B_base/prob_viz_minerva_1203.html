To solve this problem, we need to use the equation of hydrostatic equilibrium. The equation of hydrostatic equilibrium for a spherically symmetric system is given by: \[ \frac{dP}{dz} = -\rho g \] where \(P\) is the pressure, \(z\) is the height above the surface, \(\rho\) is the density, and \(g\) is the gravitational acceleration. Given that the gravitational acceleration \(g\) is constant, we can write: \[ \frac{dP}{dz} = -\rho g \] We also know the polytropic equation of state: \[ P = K \rho^{5/3} \] From this, we can express \(\rho\) in terms of \(P\): \[ \rho = \left(\frac{P}{K}\right)^{3/5} \] Substituting this into the hydrostatic equilibrium equation, we get: \[ \frac{dP}{dz} = -\left(\frac{P}{K}\right)^{3/5} g \] This is a separable differential equation. We can separate the variables and integrate: \[ \int_{P_0}^{P} P^{-3/5} \, dP = -\int_{0}^{z} g \, dz \] The left-hand side integral is: \[ \int_{P_0}^{P} P^{-3/5} \, dP = \left[ \frac{P^{2/5}}{\frac{2}{5}} \right]_{P_0}^{P} = \frac{5}{2} \left( P^{2/5} - P_0^{2/5} \right) \] The right-hand side integral is: \[ -\int_{0}^{z} g \, dz = -g z \] So we have: \[ \frac{5}{2} \left( P^{2/5} - P_0^{2/5} \right) = -g z \] Solving for \(P\), we get: \[ P^{2/5} = P_0^{2/5} - \frac{2g z}{5} \] Raising both sides to the power of \(5/2\), we obtain: \[ P = \left( P_0^{2/5} - \frac{2g z}{5} \right)^{5/2} \] This is the pressure as a function of height \(z\) above the surface of the planet.
