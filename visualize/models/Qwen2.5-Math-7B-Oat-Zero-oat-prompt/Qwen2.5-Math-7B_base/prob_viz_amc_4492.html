The total amount of juice in the four glasses is: \[ 3 \cdot \frac{J}{4} + \frac{J}{12} = \frac{3J}{4} + \frac{J}{12} = \frac{9J + J}{12} = \frac{10J}{12} = \frac{5J}{6}. \] Since the total amount of juice \( J \) is distributed equally among the four glasses, each glass should contain: \[ \frac{J}{4}. \] Currently, the fourth glass has \(\frac{J}{12}\) of juice, so it needs: \[ \frac{J}{4} - \frac{J}{12} = \frac{3J - J}{12} = \frac{2J}{12} = \frac{J}{6} \] more juice to be equal to the other glasses.
