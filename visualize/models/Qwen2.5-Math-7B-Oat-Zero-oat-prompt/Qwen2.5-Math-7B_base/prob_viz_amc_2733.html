For the cards to be picked up in exactly two passes, the first pass must pick up all the cards from the left up to some point, and the second pass must pick up the remaining cards. Let's denote the position of the last card picked up in the first pass as \( k \). This means that the first \( k \) cards in the sequence are picked up in the first pass, and the remaining \( 13 - k \) cards are picked up in the second pass. For the cards to be picked up in exactly two passes, the first \( k \) cards must be in increasing order, and the remaining \( 13 - k \) cards must also be in increasing order. The number of ways to choose the first \( k \) cards from 13 cards is given by the binomial coefficient \( \binom{13}{k} \). For each choice of \( k \), there is exactly one way to arrange the first \( k \) cards in increasing order and one way to arrange the remaining \( 13 - k \) cards in increasing order.
