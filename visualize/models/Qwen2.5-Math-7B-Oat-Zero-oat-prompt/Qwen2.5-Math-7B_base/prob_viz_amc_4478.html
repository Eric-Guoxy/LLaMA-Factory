According to the problem, this will increase her mean by 1, so we can write the equation: \[ \frac{nM + 11}{n + 1} = M + 1 \] Multiplying both sides by \( n + 1 \) gives: \[ nM + 11 = (n + 1)(M + 1) = nM + M + n + 1 \] Simplifying, we get: \[ 11 = M + n + 1 \] So: \[ M + n = 10 \quad \text{(Equation 1)} \] If Maureen scores an 11 on each of the next three quizzes, her new total sum of scores will be \( nM + 3 \times 11 = nM + 33 \) and her new number of quizzes will be \( n + 3 \).
