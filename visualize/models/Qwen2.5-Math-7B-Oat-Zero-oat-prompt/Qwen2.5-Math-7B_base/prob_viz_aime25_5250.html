To solve this problem, we need to use the properties of an isosceles trapezoid with an inscribed circle. Here are the steps: 1. **Properties of the Trapezoid:** - The trapezoid is isosceles, so the non-parallel sides are of equal length. - The circle is inscribed in the trapezoid, so the sum of the lengths of the parallel sides (bases) is equal to the sum of the lengths of the non-parallel sides. That is, \(r + s = 2a\), where \(a\) is the length of each non-parallel side. - The height of the trapezoid is equal to the diameter of the inscribed circle, which is \(2 \times 3 = 6\). 2. **Area of the Trapezoid:** The area \(A\) of the trapezoid is given by: \[ A = \frac{1}{2} \times (r + s) \times \text{height} = \frac{1}{2} \times (r + s) \times 6 = 3(r + s) \] We are given that the area is 72, so: \[ 3(r + s) = 72 \implies r + s = 24 \] From the property of the inscribed circle, we also have: \[ r + s = 2a \implies 2a = 24 \implies a = 12 \] 3. **Finding \(r\) and \(s\):** Let's denote the lengths of the parallel sides as \(r\) and \(s\) with \(r > s\). This distance is split equally on both sides, so each right triangle formed has a base of \(\frac{r - s}{2}\) and a height of 6. The hypotenuse of each right triangle is the non-parallel side \(a = 12\).
