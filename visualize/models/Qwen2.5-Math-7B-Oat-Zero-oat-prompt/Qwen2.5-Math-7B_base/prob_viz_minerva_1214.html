The observed wavelength \(\lambda_{\text{obs}}\) of a light source moving away from an observer is given by the formula: \[ \lambda_{\text{obs}} = \lambda_{\text{rest}} \left(1 + \frac{v}{c}\right) \] where \(\lambda_{\text{rest}}\) is the rest wavelength of the light, \(v\) is the speed of the galaxy moving away from us, and \(c\) is the speed of light.
