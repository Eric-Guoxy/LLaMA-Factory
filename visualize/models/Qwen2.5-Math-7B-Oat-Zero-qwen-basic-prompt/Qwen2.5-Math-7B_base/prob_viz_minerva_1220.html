The magnitude of a star is a logarithmic measure of its brightness. The formula to find the combined magnitude \(M_{\text{total}}\) of \(N\) stars, each with an individual magnitude \(M\), is given by: \[M_{\text{total}} = M - \log_{10}(N)\] Here, \(M = 5\) (the absolute magnitude of each star) and \(N = 10^6\) (the number of stars in the cluster).
