To solve this problem, let's start by defining the side length of the original square. Suppose the side length of the square is \( s \). The area of the original square is \( s^2 \). When the lengths of two opposite sides are decreased by 40%, the new length of these sides becomes \( s \times (1 - 0.40) = 0.60s \). When the lengths of the other two sides are increased by 50%, the new length of these sides becomes \( s \times (1 + 0.50) = 1.50s \). The area of the resulting rectangle is the product of these new side lengths: \( 0.60s \times 1.50s = 0.90s^2 \). The decrease in area is the original area minus the new area: \( s^2 - 0.90s^2 = 0.10s^2 \). The percent decrease in area is the decrease in area divided by the original area, multiplied by 100: \( \frac{0.10s^2}{s^2} \times 100 = 10\% \). So, the area of the square decreases by \( 10\% \). Let's confirm this with Python code. ```python # Let's define the side length of the original square as s s = 1 # The value of s does not matter as it will cancel out in the percentage calculation # New side lengths after the changes new_side1 = s * (1 - 0.40) # 60% of the original side length new_side2 = s * (1 + 0.50) # 150% of the original side length # Area of the original square original_area = s ** 2 # Area of the new rectangle new_area = new_side1 * new_side2 # Decrease in area decrease_in_area = original_area - new_area # Percent decrease in area percent_decrease = (decrease_in_area / original_area) * 100 print(percent_decrease) ``` ```output 10.0 ``` The area of the square decreases by \(\boxed{10}\) percent.
