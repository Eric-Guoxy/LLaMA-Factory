To solve this problem, we need to determine the point where Alicia and Beth meet. We know the following: 1. The distance between City \( A \) and City \( B \) is 45 miles. 2. Alicia bikes towards City \( B \) at 18 miles per hour. 3. Beth bikes towards City \( A \) at 12 miles per hour. 4. They start at the same time and meet after some duration \( t \) (in hours). First, we need to find the time \( t \) it takes for them to meet. Since they are biking towards each other, their combined speed is \( 18 + 12 = 30 \) miles per hour. The distance between them is 45 miles, so the time \( t \) it takes for them to meet is given by: \[ t = \frac{45 \text{ miles}}{30 \text{ miles per hour}} = 1.5 \text{ hours} \] Now, we need to find out how far Alicia has traveled in 1.5 hours. Since Alicia bikes at 18 miles per hour, the distance she travels in 1.5 hours is: \[ \text{Distance traveled by Alicia} = 18 \text{ miles per hour} \times 1.5 \text{ hours} = 27 \text{ miles} \] So, they will meet 27 miles from City \( A \).
