To solve this problem, we need to calculate the energy of the incident electron beam. The energy of an electron can be calculated using the formula: \[ E = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \] where \( m \) is the mass of the electron and \( v \) is its velocity. The mass of an electron is approximately \( 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \) kg. The velocity of the electrons is given as \( 4.4 \times 10^5 \) m/s. We will first calculate the energy in joules and then convert it to electron volts (eV). The conversion factor from joules to electron volts is \( 1 \text{ eV} = 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J} \). Let's calculate the energy step by step. 1. Calculate the kinetic energy of the electron in joules. 2. Convert the energy from joules to electron volts. Here is the Python code to perform these calculations: ```python # Given values mass_of_electron = 9.11e-31 # kg velocity_of_electron = 4.4e5 # m/s conversion_factor = 1.602e-19 # J/eV # Calculate the kinetic energy of the electron in joules kinetic_energy_joules = 0.5 * mass_of_electron * (velocity_of_electron ** 2) # Convert the energy to electron volts kinetic_energy_eV = kinetic_energy_joules / conversion_factor kinetic_energy_eV_int = round(kinetic_energy_eV) # Round to the nearest integer print(kinetic_energy_eV_int) ``` ```output 5 ``` The energy of the incident electron beam is \(\boxed{5}\) eV.
